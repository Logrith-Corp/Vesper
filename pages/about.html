Why we built this

The browser hasn't
changed in 20 years.

Every major browser improvement over the past two decades has been about speed, security, or extensions. None of them have changed the fundamental interaction model: you click, the browser fetches.

We think the browser should be doing cognitive work — not just network work. It should understand what you're trying to accomplish and help you get there.

That's what Vesper is. Not a faster browser. A smarter one.

2023

The Frustration

Three engineers quit their jobs at a major tech company after realizing the browser they were maintaining was fundamentally broken.

2024

First Prototype

Neural Prefetch v0.1 — a Chrome extension that predicted navigation. 2.3× speed improvement in early tests.

2025

Ground Up Rebuild

Rebuilt as a standalone browser with Chromium core. Added Context Graph and local AI inference engine.

2026

Public Beta

Vesper opens to the public. macOS first. Windows and Linux coming in Q2.

What we believe

Four principles
that drive everything.

01
⚡

Speed is respect

Making someone wait for information is a form of disrespect for their time. We treat latency as a moral problem, not just a technical one.

02
🔒

Privacy is structural

Privacy that can be removed by a policy update is not privacy. We build it into the architecture so that no future decision can break it.

03
🧠

Intelligence should disappear

The best AI is the kind you don't notice. Our goal is for Vesper to feel like your own enhanced cognition, not a separate system you interact with.

04
🌐

Open by default

Privacy claims that can't be audited are marketing. We open-source the critical components so that every claim we make can be independently verified.
